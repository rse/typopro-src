The TeX Gyre project started in 2006 and is an extensive remake and
extension of the freely available 35 base PostScript fonts distributed
with Ghostscript version 4.00.

- TeX Gyre Adventor
  based on URW Gothic L
  resembling ITC Avant Garde Gothic (by Herb Lubalin and Tom Carnase, 1970)
- TeX Gyre Bonum
  based on URW Bookman L
  resembling ITC Bookman (by Alexander Phemister, 1860, redesigned by Edward Benguiat, 1975)
- TeX Gyre Chorus
  based on URW Chancery L Medium Italic
  resembling ITC Zapf Chancery (by Hermann Zapf, 1979)
- TeX Gyre Cursor
  based on URW Nimbus Mono L
  resembling Courier (by Howard G. "Bud" Kettler, 1955, for IBM)
- TeX Gyre Heros
  based on URW Nimbus Sans L
  resembling Helvetica (aka Swiss) (by Max Miedinger and Eduard Hoffmann, 1957, at Haas Type Foundry) 
- TeX Gyre Pagella
  based on URW Palladio L
  resembling Palatino (by Hermann Zapf in the 1940's for the Stempel type foundry)
- TeX Gyre Schola
  based on URW Century Schoolbook L
  resembling Century Schoolbook (by Morris Fuller Benton, 1919, for the American Type Founders)
- TeX Gyre Termes
  based on URW Nimbus Roman No9 L
  resembling Times (new) Roman (by Stanley Morison, Starling Burgess, Victor Lardent for "The Times")
